Electronic Circuit Design

This project focused on designing and constructing an electronic circuit on a breadboard as part of my coursework in Electronic Engineering. The objective was to create a functional circuit capable of controlling an LED display using a simple input mechanism. I used a variety of components, including resistors, capacitors, transistors, and a 555 timer IC, to build a circuit that could toggle the LED on and off at a specific frequency.

The project required careful planning, including creating a schematic diagram using software like Proteus, prototyping on the breadboard, and testing the circuit for functionality and stability. The images below show the initial prototype (image1.jpg) and the final assembled circuit (image2.jpg). This project taught me the importance of precision in circuit design and deepened my understanding of analog electronics.
